Your preferences

Be first in line for the brands you actually want.

Instead of checking Trello constantly and hoping the right card shows up, you tell the sniper exactly what you're interested in — and it does the watching for you.

❤️
Watch only what you love
Add an allowlist of your favourite clients — fashion, tech, food, gaming, whatever. The sniper ignores everything else and alerts you the instant one of YOUR boards has a new card ready.
🚫
Block what you don't want
Don't want health supplements, wellness brands, or a specific client? Add any keyword to your blocklist. You'll never hear about them again — even if a dozen cards drop at once.
📅
Control your workload
Set a weekly cap so you never get overwhelmed. Hit your limit and alerts pause for the rest of the week. Raise or lower it any time.
Two common setups
You only want fashion and food brands:
"allowlist": [ "Nike Board", "Zara Q3", "HelloFresh" ]
Leave everything outside this list — the sniper won't even look at it.
You want everything except health brands:
"health_blocklist_brands": [ "Herbalife", "PEAQ", "supplement" ]
Any board whose name contains these words gets skipped automatically.
You set these in your sniper_config.json file (Option 1) or as environment variables in Railway (Option 2). Each editor has their own — your filters don't affect anyone else's.
Setup

Pick your option.

Option 1 is simpler. Option 2 works even when your laptop is off.

Option 1 — Laptop
Runs on your computer
Works when your laptop is on and connected to the internet.
1
Install Python
Open the Microsoft Store on your laptop, search Python 3.12, click Install. It's free.
2
Download the files
Go to github.com/videoaditor/Card-Sniper- → click the green Code button → Download ZIP → unzip the folder somewhere easy to find (e.g. your Desktop).
3
Create your config file
Inside the unzipped folder, find the file called sniper_config.example.json. Make a copy of it and rename the copy to sniper_config.json. Open it in Notepad.
4
Fill in your personal details
In the config file, update these four things:
"editor_label": "yournameb" "slack_user_id": "YOUR SLACK ID" "slack_token": "GET FROM #editing (see below)" "trello_token": "GENERATE BELOW"
💡 Your Slack user ID: click your name in Slack → tap the three dots (•••) → Copy member ID
💡 Your editor label is your first name + last initial + b. Not sure? Ask in the group chat.
5
Get your Trello token
Open this link in your browser and click Allow. Copy the long code it shows you and paste it as your trello_token.

Click here to get your Trello token →
6
Run the tests
Open a terminal in the folder (click the address bar in File Explorer, type cmd, press Enter). Then run:
pip install pytest python -m pytest test_card_sniper_core.py -q
You should see 30 passed. If so, everything is set up correctly.
7
Set it to start automatically
Open PowerShell (search for it in the Start menu) and paste this. Change the path if you unzipped the folder somewhere other than C:\Card Sniper:
$action = New-ScheduledTaskAction ` -Execute (Get-Command python).Source ` -Argument '"C:\Card Sniper\card_sniper.py" --loop' ` -WorkingDirectory "C:\Card Sniper" $trigger = New-ScheduledTaskTrigger -AtLogOn -User $env:USERNAME $settings = New-ScheduledTaskSettingsSet -AllowStartIfOnBatteries -DontStopIfGoingOnBatteries -StartWhenAvailable -RestartCount 5 -RestartInterval (New-TimeSpan -Minutes 2) -ExecutionTimeLimit (New-TimeSpan -Hours 0) Register-ScheduledTask -TaskName "Card Sniper" -Action $action -Trigger $trigger -Settings $settings -RunLevel Limited -Force Start-ScheduledTask -TaskName "Card Sniper"
✅ Done! The sniper will now start automatically every time you log into Windows.
Option 2 — Cloud 24/7
Runs even when laptop is off
Hosted on Railway for free. Never misses a card, day or night.
⚠️ Complete Option 1 steps 1–6 first before starting here.
1
Create a free Railway account
Go to railway.app and sign up. You can use your GitHub account to log in.
2
Create a new project
Click New project → GitHub Repository → select videoaditor/Card-Sniper- → click Deploy Now.
3
Add your personal variables
Click on the worker box → Variables tab → Raw Editor. Paste this and fill in your own details:
TRELLO_API_KEY=65440fd2bb89d722659e7aecfc7f5799 TRELLO_TOKEN=your trello token from step 5 above SLACK_TOKEN=get from #editing channel (see below) SLACK_USER_ID=your slack member ID EDITOR_LABEL=yournameb EDITOR_KEY=yourname WEEKLY_CAP=4
4
Deploy
Click Update Variables then Deploy. In about a minute you'll see the logs showing boards being scanned. You're live.

FAQ

Common questions.

What is my editor label?
It's your first name + first letter of your last name + b. For example: Maria Bauer → mariab. It's the same label used on your Trello cards. If you're not sure, check a card you've previously claimed in Trello — your label will be on it.
Where do I find my Slack user ID?
Open Slack → click on your profile picture or name → click the three dots (•••) → click Copy member ID. It looks like U0ABBMJTZ2Q.
Will I get spammed with messages?
No. The sniper only alerts you once per card and won't send anything while you already have a card in progress. You also have a weekly cap (default: 4 cards) which you can adjust in your config file.
Can I make it only alert me for the brands I actually want?
Yes — two ways. To watch only specific brands, add their board names to allowlist in your config. To block specific brands you don't want, add keywords to health_blocklist_brands — it's a partial match, so "supplement" blocks any board with that word in the name. Both filters are yours alone and don't affect other editors.
How do I stop it?
Option 1 (laptop): open PowerShell and run Stop-ScheduledTask -TaskName "Card Sniper". Option 2 (cloud): go to Railway and click the pause button on your worker service.
